Temenos delivers its Payments solution through predefined packages, with each package composed of one or more payment bundles.

This user guide helps clients:

- Understand the scope and composition of the Payments packages they have acquired.
- Identify the bundles and capabilities included in their installation.
- Understand how these bundles work together across different deployment scenarios.

## Packages and Bundles

Packages define how payments capabilities are grouped and delivered. Each package combines specific bundles based on:

- The level of core payments processing required.
- The need to support specific networks, standards, or market infrastructures, such as SWIFT or domestic clearing schemes.

## Bundle Types

The following bundle types make up the Payments Packaging Model:

- **Platform Bundles**

  Platform Bundles provide the core payments processing capabilities and form the foundation of every Payments implementation.
  There are three Platform Bundles available.
  Every client must have one Platform Bundle as a base.
- **SWIFT Bundles**

  SWIFT Bundles provide SWIFT connectivity and standards support.
  Two SWIFT Bundles are available and may be included based on the client’s SWIFT processing scope.
- **Payment Rails / Clearings**

  Payment rails and clearing bundles extend the platform to support specific domestic or scheme based payment networks.
  These bundles are optional and may be included independently, based on the client’s licensed scope.
- **Payments Repair Bundle**

  The Payments Repair bundle is an optional bundle that provides payment repair and enrichment capabilities to augment straight through processing (STP).
  It can be combined with any Platform Bundle.

## Packaging Principle

Platform Bundles provide the baseline payments capabilities, while SWIFT bundles and payment rails/clearings extend the solution to specific standards, schemes, or markets. Optional bundles enhance the solution in line with the licensed functional scope.
